# Indian-Startup-Funding-Analysis

This project explores how funding flows through the Indian startup ecosystem. Using Power BI, the dashboard brings together trends across time, industries, cities, and investment types to answer a simple question

Where is the money going and how is it evolving

---

## The Story Behind the Data

Startup funding is not evenly distributed. Some industries attract consistent investment, while others see occasional spikes. Certain cities emerge as dominant hubs, concentrating both capital and startup activity.

This dashboard uncovers these patterns by breaking the data into three key perspectives

* **Time** to understand how funding has grown or fluctuated
* **Categories** to identify which industries and investment types dominate
* **Geography** to reveal where startup activity is concentrated

---

## What the Dashboard Shows

### Overall Landscape

* Total funding highlights the scale of investment in the ecosystem
* Number of startups and cities reflects the spread of activity

### Trends Over Time

* A clear view of how funding changes year by year
* Helps identify growth phases and slowdowns

### Where the Money Goes

* Top industries receiving the highest funding
* Most funded startups driving major investments

### How Investments Are Made

* Distribution of deals across different investment types
* Shows whether early stage or later stage funding dominates

### Where Activity Happens

* Cities with the highest startup activity
* Reveals major startup hubs

---

## Data Preparation

To ensure accurate insights, the dataset was cleaned and structured

* Investment values were standardized and converted to numeric format
* Missing and inconsistent entries were handled
* Date fields were transformed to enable time based analysis
* Text fields were cleaned to avoid duplicate categories

---

## Tools Used

* Power BI for visualization
* Power Query for data transformation
* DAX for calculations

---

## Key Takeaways

* Funding is concentrated in a few industries rather than evenly spread
* A small number of cities dominate the startup ecosystem
* Investment activity varies across time, indicating cycles of growth
* Different investment types reflect varying stages of startup maturity

---
